About Me

I’m a Data Science and Computer Science student at the University of Pittsburgh with a strong foundation in mathematics and logical problem-solving. My interest in computing grew naturally from math — I’m drawn to problems where structure, efficiency, and clear reasoning matter, and where the goal is to find not just a solution, but the most effective one.

I’m especially motivated by problems involving optimization, efficiency, and sustainability. Whether I’m working with data, algorithms, or systems, I approach challenges by breaking them down mathematically, designing logical strategies, and iterating toward clean, reliable solutions. Computer science and data science appeal to me because they reward this way of thinking while also encouraging collaboration and real-world impact.

Through coursework, internships, and projects, I’ve worked with Python, Java, SQL, and R across areas such as data analysis, machine learning, and backend systems. I enjoy turning messy, unstructured data into clear insights and building tools that people can actually use. Teaching and mentoring others — through roles like peer tutoring and teaching assistance — has also strengthened how I communicate technical ideas and think about problems from multiple perspectives.

Outside of academics, I play water polo and basketball, which have shaped how I think about teamwork, discipline, and performance under pressure. I’m also involved in cultural organizations and enjoy mentoring others, experiences that reinforce my interest in working on problems that matter beyond the classroom.

Looking ahead, I see myself working as a data scientist with additional AI expertise, applying data-driven and algorithmic thinking to impactful domains such as healthcare, transportation and logistics, or sports analytics.
